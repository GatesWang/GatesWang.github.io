Claim:
Given $n+1$ points where all $x_i$ are unique

$ \begin{array}{c|cc} \text{X} & \text{Y} \\ \hline x_0 & y_0 \\ x_1 & y_1 \\ x_2 & y_2 \\ ... & ... \\ x_n & y_n \end{array} $
We can find a polynomial approximation of $n$ degree for these points .

Proof:
We want to find a polynomial such that for each $ (x_i. y_i)$ the following equation is satisfied
$a_nx_i^n + a_{n-1}x_i^{n-1} + a_{n-2}x_i^{n-2} + ... + a_1x_i + a_0 = y_i$.


In other words we want the following equations satisfied.
$a_nx_0^n + a_{n-1}x_0^{n-1} + a_{n-2}x_0^{n-2} + ... + a_1x_0 +a_0 = y_0$
$a_nx_1^n + a_{n-1}x_1^{n-1} + a_{n-2}x_1^{n-2} + ... + a_1x_1 +a_0 = y_1$
$a_nx_2^n + a_{n-1}x_2^{n-1} + a_{n-2}x_2^{n-2} + ... + a_1x_2 +a_0 = y_2$
.
.
.
$a_nx_n^n + a_{n-1}x_n^{n-1} + a_{n-2}x_n^{n-2} + ... + a_1x_n +a_0 = y_n$
Putting this system into matrix form yields
$ \begin{bmatrix} x_0^n & x_0^{n-1} & ... & x_0 & 1\\ x_1^n & x_1^{n-1} & ... & x_1 & 1\\ x_2^n & x_2^{n-1} & ... & x_2 & 1\\ \vdots \\ x_n^n & x_n^{n-1} & ... & x_n & 1\\ \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} a_{n} \\ a_{n-1} \\ \vdots \\ a_{1} \\ a_{0} \end{bmatrix} $ $ = \begin{bmatrix} y_{0} \\ y_{1} \\ y_{2} \\ \vdots \\ y_{n} \end{bmatrix} $

To prove that a solution exists we must find an inverse to our matrix.
This is a Vandermonde matrix which has a known determinant of
${ \displaystyle \prod_{1 \le i \le j \le n} (x_{j} - x_{i})}$
Since all $ x_i $ are unique, the determinant is nonzero.
